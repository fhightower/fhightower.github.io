This post serves as a list for some of the strange rules and paradigms in the English language. I plan to update it as I run across more of them.

- I never realized that English has a general [order for adjectives](http://web2.uvcs.uvic.ca/elc/studyzone/410/grammar/adjord.htm). For example, "the red, wooden, large wagon" sounds wrong (and it is incorrect according to the guidelines for adjectival ordering) while "the large, red, wooden wagon" sounds perfectly normal (and it is according to the rules).
