Most Americans don't read books anymore and it is easy to see what has caused this. There is a television in every house that is almost always on. Online services offer on-demand access any time of the day or night to more videos and TV shows than you could ever watch. When you need a break from videos, social media is always there to comfort and amuse (and isolate and uselessly fritter away countless hours of your time). Busy work schedules, friendships, and family (none of which are bad in and of themselves) also make it difficult to find the necessary tranquility and silence required to read a book. In the midst of this hubub, this post is a call to arms. Don't stop reading books! In the midst of a sea of mindless amusement, however, it can be challenging to fall in love with reading (either again or for the first time). Below is a list of engaging books that are great starting points for those of you who would like to take up this challenge and enjoy reading more.

Recommended reading list for those looking to learn how to read again:

- [Amusing Ourselves to Death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amusing_Ourselves_to_Death) - This book may as well be the manifesto of this blog post. In it, Neil Postman argues that any means of communication with inherently sets boundaries on what can be communicated. The modern wave of visual communication available via the television and the internet changes not only *how* we communicate, but also *what* we communicate about. This is a fascinating read that is well worth your time (it's short too).
- [Fahrenheit 451](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fahrenheit_451) - if you have never read this, or if it has been a while since you read it (probably in middle school), you should revisit this classic. It is well-written and will make you want to value books more (which *hopefully* leads to you reading them more).
- [A Guide to Civilized Loafing](https://openlibrary.org/books/OL6309832M/A_guide_to_civilized_loafing) - Published in 1934, this delightful book challenges readers to make the most of their free time by investing in things which profit and grow the body, mind, and soul.
- [Till We Have Faces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Till_We_Have_Faces) - This book is a retelling of the myth of Cupid and Psyche. The author, C. S. Lewis, considered this to be his best work and his friend, J. R. R. Tolkien, agreed. This book is marvelously written and is one of those rare gems such that, once I had finished reading it, I couldn't do anything for about half an hour because of the force of the work. This book is an absolute masterpiece and is beautifully executed.
